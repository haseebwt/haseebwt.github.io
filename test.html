â€” Habitat!

These silly goobers are native to the freshwater of Lake Xochimilco and Lake Chalco. However, unfortunately Lake Chalco no longer exists (Fs in the chat) due to flooding and Lake Xochimilco only exists in the form of canals. This led to their species being endangered. Now almost all living axolotls are in captivity where they strive.

â€” Types of Axolotls!

Axolotls have four pigmentation which are dependant on mutation. The most common mutant colors are:

  1. Leucistic: pale pink with black eyes.
  2. Albino: pale pink or white, with red eyes.
  3. Xanthic: grey, with black eyes.
  4. Melanoid: all black or dark blue with no gold speckling or olive tone.
  5. Wild Axolotls: brown-ish with black eyes.
There is a wide variability in size, frequency and intensity of the gold speckling, and almost at least one variant starts to develop a black and white pigment upon reaching maturity.

â€” Threat and Captivity!

As the article has mentioned, axolotls are a Critically Endangered species. We should do everything in power to protect these cute little silly billies. The population density of axolotls in the wild has dropped a significant amount in the last few decades. All the axolotls shown in this article are in captivity and great health.

If you or anyone you know is planning on adopting one of these precious little babies, we encourage you to please conduct your research and learn about all the things you need to keep them healthy. As humans and habitants of planet earth, we should try to protect the species that makes our green planet a little bit more greener and beautiful. There are a lot of articles on the internet that can lead you in the right direction and we encourage you to check them out.
